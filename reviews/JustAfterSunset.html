"Just After Sunset" by Stephen King

TL;DR

A collection of short stories by Stephen King. Some are pretty good, some are not so great. None of them were truly great to me, but many were enjoyable. Very middle of the road.
Advisory: Violence, Language

Review

Just After Sunset is a collection of 13 short stories and novellas that cover a variety of topics - ghosts, serial killers, bad dreams, cosmic monsters, domestic abuse, terrorists, devil cats, miracles, and being stuck in a porta potty (which was both amusing and absolutely disgusting). None of these stories stood out to me as being particularly amazing, my favorites were The Gingerbread Girl (a classic thriller-type story of being kidnapped and trying to escape, nothing special but very intense) and N. (dealing with a sort of Lovecraftian cosmic being). My least favorites were Harvey's Dream and Graduation Afternoon, both for the same reason - the whole story is spent setting a scene and then it all ends abruptly, leaving me unsatisfied. Overall an average book, read if it interests you.
