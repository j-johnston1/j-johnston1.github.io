"The Color Purple" by Alice Walker

Premise: The book is the story of the life of Celie, an abused African American woman in Georgia during the early 1900s, as she struggles for empowerment in her seemingly powerless predicament. Told through the letters she writes to God and her sister, Celie embarks on a mostly internal journey that leads her to discover both a courage within herself and a version of God that help her push through the sexism and racism she experiences to find fulfillment.

TL;DR

Alice Walker's Pulitzer Prize-winning classic deserves all the praise it gets for its portrayal of sexism, racism, faith, and empowerment. At the same time, parts of it can be incredibly difficult to read because of the harrowing nature of the content. I really enjoyed the book and Celie's journey of self-perception, but the slower plot and heavy content would make me hesitant to recommend to everyone, especially sensitive readers. I would encourage people to read this important book, but perhaps not everyone is ready for it.
Advisory: Sexual Content, Violence, Some Language
