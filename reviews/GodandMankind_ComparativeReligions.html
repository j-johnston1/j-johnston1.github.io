"God and Mankind: Comparative Religions" by Robert Oden

TL;DR

Professor Oden is an entertaining lecturer but failed to really draw me into the subject matter in these 6 hours of lectures. The course mostly covers different approaches for studying religion but nothing about this course really makes it stand out among the others provided by Great Courses.

Review

Professor Oden begins the lecture series discussing the questions he will cover in his lectures:
1. What is religion?
2. Why is it worth studying?
3. How can we study it?
He flies through the first two questions and then spends most of the time discussing several academic approaches to the study of religion (religion as primitive science or an explanation of natural phenomenon vs religion as a social system for building a cohesive society, for example). He also provides a brief comparison of religious themes and how the converge and diverge across the different religious traditions, focusing on stories of cosmogenesis and heroes in the various traditions. The lectures focus heavily on the Judeo-Christian religions with much briefer forays into Hinduism, Buddhism, and Islam. I believe the topic is really too vast to be covered in such a short period of time. The course would serve as a good introduction to individuals looking to begin the study of religion but does not dive into the topic deep enough for those who already have a grounding in religious studies.
