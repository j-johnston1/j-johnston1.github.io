"Language A to Z" by John McWhorter

TL;DR

A quick and fun introduction to a variety of linguistic topics. McWhorter's passion for the subject really comes through despite each lecture only being ~15 minutes. I think Great Courses should consider more short-lecture style courses, each lecture managed to begin by peaking my curiosity and end by satisfying it all in an easily digestible 15-minute morcel. Enjoyable for anyone that likes to think about language and the variety of the topics means there should be at least one unfamiliar topic in the mix.

Review

In this quick and fun lecture series, Dr. McWhorter leads us through an alphabet of linguistic topics, each topic lasting around 15 minutes. Some of the lectures were on specific languages, like A for Aramaic or Y for Yiddish. Other topics covered language structures like D for Double Negatives or C for Compounds. And some topics covered demographic differences in speech like O for Oldsters in Cartoons or B for Baby Mama. Several of the lectures covered topics about Black English, McWhorter's area of expertise, and his passion for the subjects really showed through, made me interested in paying more attention to how people talk. I find linguistics to be fascinating and really enjoyed the series. If you are also interested in linguistic topics I'm sure you would enjoy it as well.
