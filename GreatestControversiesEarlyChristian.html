"The Greatest Controversies of Early Christian History" by Bart Ehrman

TL;DR

Bart Ehrman takes us through a series of fascinating lectures, each one covering a question debated by the early Christians and often still debated to this day. He walks through the historical evidence and logical conclusions historians draw from the evidence to best answer the questions in each lecture. Some of the questions are more hotly debated than others, but all of them had interesting concerns at the heart of the debate. Well-crafted and fast-paced, this is one of my new favorite Great Courses series.

Review

Ehrman does it again in perhaps my favorite of the many series he has created for The Great Courses. If, like me, you have dived into early Christian history before, then there may not be much new here to discover. Ehrman has covered a lot of these topics himself in other lecture series he has done. Having many of the most interesting subjects covered in one course, however, makes for a fun and exciting introduction to the material for newcomers and remains a fun and exciting review for those who have heard it before. I can't recommend this course enough for people interested in the study of Christianity. For a list of the questions covered in each lecture, just check out the lecture titles on the website for The Great Courses: https://www.thegreatcourses.com/courses/the-greatest-controversies-of-christian-history
